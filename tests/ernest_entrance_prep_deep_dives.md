## Deep Dive 1 — Conditionals: the "would have" trap 🎯

*(Your biggest fixable thing. An examiner spots this instantly.)*

**What happened on the test:**
- "If I had more time, I would **have learnt** to code." ← you wrote
- "If I were rich, I would **have bought** a new computer." ← you wrote

Your *if*-part was perfect both times. The problem was only the second half — you added **have**.

**The rule — it's about TIME:**

| Situation | If-part | Result-part | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Imaginary **now / future** (2nd conditional) | past simple | **would + verb** | If I **had** time, I **would learn** to code. |
| Imaginary **past** — didn't happen (3rd conditional) | had + V3 | **would have + V3** | If I **had had** time last year, I **would have learned** to code. |

So **"would have + V3" is only for the past** — for things that are already over and can't change. "If I were rich" is about now, so it's just **"I would buy"**, not "would have bought."

**Russian check:** Russian uses one form — *бы + past* — for both. English splits it by time, so you have to ask: *am I imagining now, or regretting the past?*

**One more:** never put **will** or **would** in the *if*-part. "If I **finish** my homework..." (not *will finish*).

**Practice — fix or complete (write the whole sentence):**
1. If I were you, I would have chosen the guitar. *(you mean: now / generally)*
2. If it will rain tomorrow, we will stay home.
3. Если бы у меня был брат, я бы играл с ним в футбол. *(→ English)*
4. If she had studied harder last year, she would pass the exam.
5. If I lived in Kazakhstan, I would have skied every winter. *(you mean: now / generally)*
6. Если бы я знал ответ, я бы тебе сказал. *(→ English, about now)*

## Deep Dive 2 — Articles: "a" you shouldn't add, "the" you shouldn't drop

*(Your longest-standing weak spot — worth the most careful attention.)*

**What happened:**
- "teacher gave me **a** good advice" ← *advice* can't take *a*
- In your writing: "...prefer **climate**", "continuing **IGCSE system**" ← both needed **the**

Two different mistakes, opposite directions:

**A) Uncountable nouns take NO "a" and NO plural -s.**
These are ideas/substances you can't count one-by-one:
> *advice, information, news, money, furniture, homework, progress, weather, research, knowledge*

- ✗ a good advice → ✓ good advice / some good advice
- ✗ informations → ✓ information
- ✗ a good progress → ✓ good progress

**B) Use "the" when the thing is specific — already known, or the only one here.**
- "the climate" — you mean *the climate of Kazakhstan*, a specific one → **the**
- "the IGCSE system" — the particular system you're joining → **the**
- "the best student", "the exam we're taking" — specific → **the**

**Russian check:** Russian has no articles, so two opposite errors leak in — you *add* "a" where English uses nothing (uncountables), and you *drop* "the" where English needs it (specific things). When you write, pause on every noun: *is it countable? is it a specific one?*

**Practice — fix or complete:**
1. My teacher gave us an important information about the exam.
2. I need to buy a new furniture for my room.
3. Climate in Kazakhstan is colder than in Bahrain. *(specific climate)*
4. She made a good progress in maths this term.
5. Учитель объяснил домашнее задание. *(→ English; "the teacher", specific homework)*
6. I want to study computer science at a new school I'm joining. *(specific school)*
7. He gave me a very useful advices.
8. News about the move was a surprise. *(specific news you just heard)*

## Deep Dive 3 — Present perfect: "for / since" and "have you ever"

**What happened:**
- "I know him **since three years old**." ← two problems: wrong tense + since/for mix-up

**The rule — have / has + V3 (the third form):**

Use it for:
- **Experience**, no time said: *Have you ever been to Almaty? I have played this game before.*
- **Something that started in the past and is still true now** — with **for** (a length) or **since** (a starting point):
  - **for** + how long: *for three years, for a week, for ten minutes*
  - **since** + when it started: *since 2021, since Monday, since I was ten*

So: ✗ *I know him since three years old* → ✓ **I have known him for three years** (a length of time), or ✓ *since we were ten* (a starting point).

**Russian check:** Russian says this in the *present* — *Я знаю его три года* ("I know him three years"). English needs **have known** + **for**. This is the #1 tense trap for Russian speakers — whenever you see "for X years/months up to now", reach for *have/has + V3*.

**Practice — fix or complete:**
1. I live in Bahrain since 2015.
2. We know each other for a long time.
3. Ты когда-нибудь пробовал играть на гитаре? *(→ English)*
4. She is my best friend since primary school.
5. I have this bike for two years and it still works.
6. Я знаю его с детства. *(→ English)*

## How to prepare in the next few days

You're at **82%** with strengths that are genuinely solid. This is not a rebuild — it's closing three specific leaks. The right approach for *you*, based on how you tested:

**5 principles**

1. **Practise by writing, not choosing.** You already pass multiple-choice — your gap is producing it yourself. So every practice item = write the full sentence. No A/B/C drills.
2. **Learn from your own mistakes.** The worked examples above are *your* sentences. Re-reading why they were wrong sticks better than new rules.
3. **Short and daily beats one long cram.** ~25–30 min a day, **one topic per day**. Tired brains don't build grammar.
4. **One timed mini-write every day** (6–8 sentences, ~8 min) — then check *only* three things: **a/the**, **verb tense**, **conditionals**. Ignore everything else so it doesn't feel endless.
5. **Build an exam habit: proofread twice.** In the real test, leave 3–4 minutes at the end. One pass reading *only* for a/the. A second pass reading *only* for verb tenses. These two passes alone would have caught most of what you missed.

**A 4-day plan** (compress if you have fewer days — Day 1 and Day 2 matter most):

| Day | Focus | Do |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Conditionals (Deep Dive 1) | Read + practice. Timed write: *"If you could change one thing about your new school, what would it be?"* |
| **2** | Articles (Deep Dive 2) | Read + practice. Timed write: *describe your room* (forces lots of a/the). |
| **3** | Present perfect (Deep Dive 3) | Read + practice. Timed write: *"Three things you have done this year and how long you have had your favourite thing."* |
| **4** (test eve) | Mixed review | **Re-take the original 22-item test** as a progress check — compare to 82%. Then rest. **No new material the night before.** |

**Don't** re-study prepositions, word order, plurals/agreement, or basic past tense — you scored 100% on all of them. Time spent there is time stolen from the three that matter.
