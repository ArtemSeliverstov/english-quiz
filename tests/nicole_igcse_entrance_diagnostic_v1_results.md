## Data-quality note: two avoidance responses

Items 11 (articles) and 15 (reflexive calque) were answered by **rewording around** the target error rather than fixing it directly — "most students" instead of "most of the students," and "I feel etter [better?] when I dance" instead of fixing "I feel myself happy." Both are valid English, but neither tells us whether the underlying trap is still live. This is the same avoidance signature documented in the parent Russian-L1 diagnostic methodology (a speaker routes around a structure they're unsure of). **Recommendation**: for these two specific traps, use RU→EN translation next time — it's harder to dodge than error-correction, where rewording is always an escape hatch.
