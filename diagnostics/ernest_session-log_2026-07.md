## Session 1 — Tutor (external), 2026-07-02

Live conversation session with Ernest's English tutor. Source: `2026 Jul 2 - Ernest.docx`. Corrections captured:

| Error | Correct | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| could have **went** | could have **gone** | V3 (not V2) after *have* |
| spend time **at home** | spend time **on it** at home | dropped object / preposition |
| the school I'm going to **go** | ...to **go to** | stranded preposition kept |

**Read:** corrections cluster on **small function words** — prepositions dropped or misplaced (*to, on*) and *say/tell*. Trickier than the basic-preposition items the diagnostic scored 100% on (verb-complement + stranded prepositions). Plus one V3-after-*have* slip that ties directly to his conditional gap.

## Session 3 — CC practice set (Claude Code), 2026-07-07

Ran the [practice set](ernest_practice-set_2026-07-07.md) as cold **production** (full sentences, no MCQ). Stopped after Section E by his call.

| Section | Result |
|---|---|
| A — have + V3 | 4/5 — *tooken* → **taken** (irregular participle, not the rule) |
| B — say/tell | 3/3 choices ✓ — flagged reported-speech backshift (*told me … will* → *would*) |
| C — small word | 3/4 — *explain **for** me* → *explain **to** me* (the *explain + to* leak persists from Session 1) |
| D — gerund after prep | **5/5 grammar ✓** — the last undrilled leak is now closing; one vocab slip (*opening* → *starting* for *начало*) |
| E — proofread drill | 5/9 on first real pass |

**Headline finding — article *production* is the live gap, and it's specifically *missing "the"*.** Ernest reliably catches a wrong *a* (*a good advice* → uncountable) but does **not** add a missing *the* when a sentence opens with a bare noun (*Climate…* → *The climate…*; *It is school* → *It is the school*). This matches the profile's "recognition vs production" note — recognition is fine, production of *the* is not yet reflexive.

Secondary: when overcorrecting he **introduced** errors on already-correct text (*than here* → *than in here*; *excited* → *exciting*). Coached the rule "if it already reads fine, leave it." Tense and small-word passes were solid (*could have gone*, *would study*, *go to*, *to us*, *on my phone*).

**Read:** grammar floor is holding; the clearest remaining exam cost is now **article production (add-the)**, not the four items that seeded the practice set. Next sitting per the weekly plan = Reading task (§1 of the [prep pack](ernest_igcse-english-prep_year11.md)).
