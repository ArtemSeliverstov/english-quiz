# Ernest — Practice Set (tomorrow, ~30 min)

Built from the tutor session (2026-07-02) + CC drill (2026-07-06). Targets the patterns that actually slipped. See [session log](ernest_session-log_2026-07.md).

**One rule for everything: write the full sentence. No picking letters — your gap is producing, not recognising.** Cover each answer key until you're done.

---

## A. The "have + V3" habit — put the bracketed verb in its 3rd form
1. We could have ___ (go) to the museum, but it was closed.
2. If she had studied harder, she would have ___ (pass) the exam.
3. Have you ever ___ (see) snow?
4. I have ___ (know) him since we were five.
5. They could have ___ (take) the earlier bus.

*Key: 1. gone 2. passed 3. seen 4. known 5. taken*

## B. say vs tell — fill in the right one
1. He ___ me that the exam is on Monday.
2. She ___ that she was tired.
3. Can you ___ me the answer?
4. *Учитель сказал мне, что тест будет лёгким.* → English

*Key: 1. told 2. said 3. tell 4. The teacher told me (that) the test would be easy.*

## C. The small word after the verb — add or fix
1. Can you explain the homework ___ me?
2. It's the school I'm going to go ___.
3. I spend a lot of time ___ my phone after dinner.
4. She explained the rules ___ us.

*Key: 1. to 2. to 3. on 4. to*

## D. Gerund after a preposition — the last leak
> **Rule:** after a preposition (*to, of, at, about, on*), the next verb takes **-ing**. This *to* is a preposition, not the "to" of *to go / to eat*.
1. I look forward to ___ (meet) my new classmates.
2. I'm good at ___ (solve) maths problems.
3. He's used to ___ (get) up early.
4. Instead of ___ (watch) TV, I studied.
5. *Я с нетерпением жду начала в новой школе.* → *I look forward to ___ at the new school.*

*Key: 1. meeting 2. solving 3. getting 4. watching 5. starting*

## E. Proofreading drill — find and report

8 sentences below contain **exactly 9 mistakes — 3 for each pass.** Do one pass at a time, hunting for *only* that type, and don't stop until you've found all 3. Then check the key.

1. My teacher gave me a good advice about the exam.
2. She explained the rules us very clearly.
3. Last year I could have went to a summer camp, but I was sick.
4. I have known my best friend since we are five years old.
5. I spend a lot of time my phone, so now I want to study more.
6. If I had more time, I would studied every evening.
7. Climate in Kazakhstan is colder than here.
8. It is school I am going to go, and I am really excited.

| Pass | Hunt for ONLY this | How many | Write each fix (sentence # → correction) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Articles — *a / the* | **3** | ___ ___ ___ |
| **2** | Verb tense — incl. *have + V3*, *would* + base | **3** | ___ ___ ___ |
| **3** | Small word after a verb — *to, on…* | **3** | ___ ___ ___ |

If a box has fewer than 3, keep looking. ⚠️ **Sentence 8 hides two different kinds** — don't stop after the first.

**Key —**
Pass 1 (articles): S1 *a good advice* → good advice · S7 *Climate* → The climate · S8 *It is school* → the school
Pass 2 (tense): S3 *could have went* → could have gone · S4 *since we are five* → since we were five · S6 *would studied* → would study
Pass 3 (small word): S2 *explained the rules us* → to us · S5 *time my phone* → time on my phone · S8 *going to go* → going to go to

## F. Free write (8 min) — then proofread your OWN writing

Write **6–8 sentences**: *"If you could design your perfect school, what would it be like?"*

Then run the **same three passes on your own text**, one at a time. After each pass, **write down every fix you made** in the box — same as the drill above. This proves you actually looked, not just glanced.

| Pass | Hunt for ONLY this | Write each fix you made (or "none found") |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | Articles — *a / the* | ___ |
| **2** | Verb tense — incl. *have + V3*, *would* + base | ___ |
| **3** | Small word after a verb — *to, on…* | ___ |

If a pass finds **"none,"** read that sentence once more before you believe it — clean-looking writing usually still hides one. This is the exact habit for the real exam: leave 3–4 minutes at the end and do these passes one at a time.
