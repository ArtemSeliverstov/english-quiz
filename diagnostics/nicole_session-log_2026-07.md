## The through-line

Most of Nicole's errors share one root: **the auxiliary / "be" system and verb-form agreement.** Drop *is/are*, drop 3rd-person *-s*, mis-handle *did + verb*, miss irregular 3rd forms. These aren't five separate problems — they're one under-built area: **making the verb agree and keeping the small "be"/auxiliary word in.** Good news: fixing the core rule (*he/she/it → verbS; every sentence needs a "be" or a main verb*) cleans up several surfaces at once.

## Consolidated priorities (she has a full year of runway)

1. **Verb agreement + "be"** — the through-line. *He walks / She is happy / They are here.* Highest leverage.

3. **much / many** and **the** for specific things — reconfirmed, lighter touch.
