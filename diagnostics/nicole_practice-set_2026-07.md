# Nicole — Practice Set (~25 min)

Built from her tutor session (2026-07-04) + diagnostic (2026-07-01). Targets her **one big pattern**: keeping the *"be"* word in and making verbs **agree**. See [session log](nicole_session-log_2026-07.md).

**One rule: write the whole sentence.** Don't just pick — *make* the sentence. Cover each key until you're done.

---

## Warm-up — tell me about your music 🎶
*(No right or wrong here — just write 2–3 sentences to get going.)*
What music do you really like, and who are your favourite singers or bands? What do you like about them? **Write about what *you* like — any genre or language.**

---

## A. He / she / it → verb**S**
*I, we, you, they → plain verb. He, she, it (and one person/thing) → add **-s**.*
1. My best friend ___ (love) K-pop, but I like Russian music more.
2. She ___ (play) badminton every Saturday.
3. My best friend ___ (live) in Bahrain.
4. Nobody ___ (need) that in the game.
5. My favourite band ___ (make) really good music.

*Key: 1. loves 2. plays 3. lives 4. needs 5. makes*

## B. Keep the "be" word in (*is / are / am*)
*Every sentence needs a "be" word or a main verb — don't drop it.*
1. This not a problem.
2. Look! It raining outside.
3. My friends here now.
4. I really tired today.
5. Nobody using it, so we can take it.

*Key: 1. This **is** not a problem. 2. It **is** raining. 3. My friends **are** here. 4. I **am** really tired. 5. Nobody **is** using it.*

## C. After *did / didn't* → plain verb
*The "did" already shows it's past — so the next verb stays plain.*
1. I didn't ___ (went) to school yesterday.
2. She didn't ___ (liked) the movie.
3. Did you ___ (saw) that?
4. We didn't ___ (bought) any food.

*Key: 1. go 2. like 3. see 4. buy*

## D. The tricky one — irregular 3rd form (*go / went / gone*)
Every verb has **three** forms. Use the **3rd form after have / has**:
> go → went → **gone** · sing → sang → **sung** · drive → drove → **driven** · eat → ate → **eaten** · see → saw → **seen**

Complete with the **3rd form**:
1. I have never ___ (drive) a car.
2. Have you ___ (see) the new BTS video?
3. She has ___ (sing) that song a hundred times.
4. We have ___ (eat) all the canned food.
5. My family has ___ (go) to Almaty.

*Key: 1. driven 2. seen 3. sung 4. eaten 5. gone*

## E. much / many + *the*
*Count them? → **many** (people, apples). Can't count? → **much** (water, food). Use **the** for the one specific/only thing.*
1. There are too ___ people at the concert.
2. I bought too ___ apples.
3. Almaty is ___ biggest city in Kazakhstan.
4. I play games at ___ weekend.

*Key: 1. many 2. many 3. the (biggest) 4. the (at the weekend)*

## F. Proofreading drill — find and report

8 sentences, **exactly 9 mistakes — 3 for each pass.** Do one pass at a time, hunt for *only* that type, and don't stop until you've found all 3.

1. My best friend loves zombie games at weekend.
2. We playing every weekend after school.
3. There are too much zombies in the city.
4. I have never see a level this hard.
5. My little brother scared, but I am not.
6. She always bring too much snacks.
7. This the best game in the world.
8. We have eat all the food already.

| Pass | Hunt for ONLY this | How many | Write each fix (sentence # → correction) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | The **"be" word** — *is / are / am* | **3** | ___ ___ ___ |
| **2** | **Verb form** — *he/she/it + s*, and *have + 3rd form* | **3** | ___ ___ ___ |
| **3** | **much / many** and **the** | **3** | ___ ___ ___ |

⚠ **Sentence 6 hides two** — don't stop after the first.

**Key —**
Pass 1 (be): S2 *We* **are** *playing* · S5 *brother* **is** *scared* · S7 *This* **is** *the best*
Pass 2 (verb form): S4 *have never* **seen** · S6 *She always* **brings** · S8 *have* **eaten**
Pass 3 (much/many/the): S1 *at* **the** *weekend* · S3 *too* **many** *zombies* · S6 *too* **many** *snacks*

## G. Free write (8 min) — then check it

Write **6–8 sentences** about your **zombie-survival team**: who is in it, what each person does, and three things you would take. *(This makes you use "he does / she fixes" and "many / much" a lot.)*

Then run the **same three passes on your own writing**, one at a time. After each pass, write down what you changed:

| Pass | Hunt for ONLY this | Write each fix you made (or "none found") |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | The "be" word — *is / are / am* | ___ |
| **2** | Verb form — *he/she/it + s*, *have + 3rd form* | ___ |
| **3** | *much / many* and *the* | ___ |

If a pass finds **"none,"** read that sentence once more before you believe it — clean-looking writing usually still hides one.
