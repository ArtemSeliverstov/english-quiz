## Recommended next-cycle priorities (ranked)

1. **🔴 Conditional result-clause form** ("would have Ved" over-generalisation). New finding, cheap to fix, high visibility risk in a written entrance exam. Single focused session: contrast "if + past simple → would + verb" (2nd conditional) against "if + past perfect → would have + Ved" (3rd conditional), with his own two sentences as the worked examples.
2. **⚠ Articles — uncountable nouns taking "a."** Confirmed stable pattern (advice, informations-family). Matches existing weak_patterns note — no new discovery here, just confirmation it's still live going into the exam. Suggest folding this diagnostic's evidence into his `coach_notes.weak_patterns` (see note below).
3. **⚠ Present perfect + for/since (duration).** One clean miss on a B2 item, everything else in tense/aspect solid. A short, focused drill on "have/has + past participle + for/since" — not a full tense-system review.
4. **Definite-article drop on specific/known nouns** (the climate, the IGCSE system) — same family as #2, surfaced spontaneously in free writing. Bundle into the articles session rather than treating separately.
5. **Not urgent**: gerund after "look forward to" — one B2 stretch item, core B1 gerund set is fine. Low priority; a single example correction would likely fix it.
