## Diagnosis — one phenomenon, two symptoms

Artem's English was acquired through business reading and documents. That produced C1 comprehension and writing inside one band — neutral-formal written — and almost nothing outside it:

- **Production symptom**: spoken interaction defaults to document register. Hedging, turn-taking, pushback, response tokens, numbers-talk — the machinery of meetings and calls — is the absent variety. Evidence: Register worst quiz category (40%), PV production gap, calque-prone free-write, `register_rubric` data drought until CR1.
- **Reception symptom**: transparent prose (Sanderson, Murderbot) reads easily; The Black Company — military slang, ellipsis, irony, allusion, voice-heavy narration — resists. Same missing band, receptive side.

The track widens the band in both directions: **produce spoken-professional** (Lane A) and **decode register-marked prose** (Lane B). The two feed each other — grimdark dialogue is a supply line of exactly the informal chunks his speech lacks.

Content knowledge is not the gap: 20+ yrs finance/strategy (Big 4 → McKinsey → TNK-BP → CFO roles → Bapco), US CPA. He knows what a covenant-breach call contains; the track teaches only the language-interaction layer over scenarios he owns cold. That asymmetry is the engagement asset — lessons never explain finance to a CFO.

### Session anatomy — lesson day

1. **Warm-up talk** — real life (job search, F1, news), audio drop preferred 1–2×/wk via the shipped `/v1/audio` flow; 1–2 register swaps captured, no lecturing.
2. **Teacher block** — the "интересно" core. Narrative mini-lecture on one interaction mechanic: how CFOs actually soften bad news, what an MD says when a covenant slips, how natives interrupt without being rude. Always built on contrast pairs — *document-English version vs what a native says* — his exact profile. Ends with 3–5 takeaway chunks.
3. **Drill block** — one front, 10+ items, strictly one at a time; today's chunks to production (phrase-swap / particle / transform mechanics) interleaved with ▲-zone grammar per domain map; adaptive sibling protocol on misses.
4. **Live scenario** — roleplay, coach plays the counterpart (bank MD, EY partner, McKinsey EM, recruiter, CEO); engineered pushback moments; audio-first when practical. Feedback tier `light`.
5. **Wrap** — chunk recap, Тайминг line, log, optional register-check / prompt-rephrasing offer.

### Reading Lab day

Artem brings one passage that resisted (≤1 page, his own copy). Decode together: who's speaking to whom, what's unsaid, which words mark register, which allusions carry weight. Then the production twist — retell the scene twice: hallway version and board version (bridges reception to production). Harvest 3–5 chunks into the tracker. Logs carry the chunk list and observations, never extended excerpts.
