# Articles — research basis

Citations that ground article work — drills, exp fields, weak-spots ladders. Distilled from a 2026-04 SLA-literature consolidation; numbers in that doc were stale, findings below are not.

## Fluctuation Hypothesis — Ionin, Ko & Wexler 2004

Article-less-L1 learners (Russian, Chinese, Korean) fluctuate between two parameters: definiteness (English's system) and specificity (does the speaker have a particular referent in mind). Errors concentrate in two mismatch cells:

| Cell | Target | Russian error | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| [+def, +spec] | the | usually ✓ | parameters converge |
| [+def, −spec] | the | uses *a* | non-specific reads as indefinite |
| [−def, +spec] | a | uses *the* | specific reads as definite |
| [−def, −spec] | a | usually ✓ | parameters converge |

Trenkic 2007 offered the alternative Syntactic Misanalysis account (articles treated as adjectives), explaining the omission spike with pre-modified NPs.

## Master's binary system — Master 1990, TESOL Quarterly

Most effective article-teaching framework (Master 2002 comparative study against three rivals).

| Operation | Question | Article |
|---|---|---|
| Classification | What kind? | *a* (sg count) / ∅ (pl, mass) |
| Identification | Which one? | *the* |

Maps directly to the "Do we both know which one?" heuristic.

## Discourse dependence — Brown 1983, Jonz 1990

52–70% of cloze items involve cohesion. 32% of article blanks require cross-sentence information. Jarvis 2002: articles drop preferentially in topic-continuation positions — precisely where *the* is needed.

## REALEC corpus — HSE Moscow

6,054 Russian learner essays · 134,608 error annotations:

| Error type | Share |
|---|---:|
| Incorrect *the* (overuse/misuse) | 54% |
| Article omission | 37% |
| Incorrect *a* | 9% |

Russian speakers misselect; they don't primarily omit. Derkach & Alexopoulou 2024: 55% accuracy on mass nouns.

## L1 scaffolds — with limits

| Russian | Maps to | Limit |
|---|---|---|
| этот (this) | the (definite, present) | ~60% of *the* contexts |
| тот (that) | the (definite, anaphoric) | breaks for structural *the* (the UK, play the piano) |
| один (one / a certain) | a (specific indefinite) | doesn't cover non-specific *a* |
| какой-нибудь (any) | a (non-specific) | reasonable |
| Word order (preverbal = known) | the | fails entirely in English SVO |

Use at B1 only. At B2+ the bridges mislead on generic, structural, and conventional *the*.

## Fossilisation — Ionin & Díez-Bedmar 2021

Russian speakers do **not** show the B1→B2 improvement curve Spanish speakers do — article errors plateau. Chrabaszcz & Jiang 2014 confirmed after years of immersion. Definite singular generics (*the whale is endangered*), articles in pre-modified NPs, and abstract-noun articles are C1 — do not introduce prematurely.
